I am a problem solver . Coming from a family that is actively engaged in basic science research at a few different universities as I was growing up , I have always come at problems with a curious mind and learned the scientific method at an early age . And , having to move with my parents , I have lived in multiple cities in and outside of the United States , have been exposed to several perspectives which I now realized that has served me well . When I majored in biology and biomedical engineering , I realized that it is the human body that fascinated me and decided to apply to medical school . I have excelled in medical school and each course has been a special treat to learn more about anatomy and each body system . I enjoyed all my rotations in medical school , which has made deciding my residency plans difficult , but have been seeking one that brought me to the pinnacle of problem solving . After long consideration , I have decided that no other specialty I have observed in medical school has had more of a role in problem solving than radiology . The breadth of knowledge that is required to come up with a good differential diagnosis in so many areas is very appealing to me , along with the potential for hands on procedures .
